MultiFOLD – Results Help Guide

Use this guide to interpret prediction confidence, colouring, and scores.

Key Information

Chain Coloring

  • Blue: Accurate prediction
  • Red: Low confidence

Score Meanings

These scores help assess model confidence, structural accuracy, and interface quality. Most scores range from 0 to 1, where values closer to 1 indicate higher confidence or better predicted quality. Some scores, such as pLDDT, use a different scale.

Assembly Score

Reflects confidence in inter-chain interactions and structure assembly. Higher values suggest greater confidence in how the chains are arranged together.

Interface Score

Indicates confidence in predicted protein-protein interfaces. Higher values suggest greater confidence in the predicted contact regions between chains.

ModFOLDIA

Overall model quality score for multimer predictions. Higher values indicate greater overall confidence.

pLDDT

Per-residue confidence score that indicates how reliable the prediction is for local regions of the structure.

  • 90–100: Very high confidence
  • 70–90: High confidence
  • 50–70: Medium confidence
  • <50: Low confidence

pTM

Predicted Template Modelling score. It estimates confidence in the overall fold or global structure. Values closer to 1 indicate higher confidence in the predicted structure.

ipTM

Interface predicted TM score. It estimates confidence in the relative positioning of different chains. Values closer to 1 indicate more reliable placement of chains within a complex.

pDockQ

Predicted DockQ score for protein-protein docking quality. Values closer to 1 indicate higher confidence in the predicted interface.

pDockQ2

Updated DockQ-based score that evaluates interface quality with additional confidence information. Values closer to 1 indicate more reliable predicted interfaces.

Cons_IF

Consensus interface score. Values closer to 1 indicate stronger agreement and higher confidence in the interface region.

Cons_fold

Consensus fold score. Values closer to 1 indicate more consistent predicted folds.

Cons_total

Combined consensus score. Values closer to 1 indicate higher overall consistency and confidence.

globalIF

Global interface quality score. Values closer to 1 indicate higher confidence in interface organisation.

Missing scores

A dash or empty value indicates that the score was not calculated or is not available.

FAQ

What does a high pTM score mean?

A pTM score ranges from 0 to 1. Values closer to 1 indicate higher confidence in the overall predicted structure.

Why are some regions red?

Red regions indicate low confidence predictions (pLDDT < 50).

How should Assembly Score be interpreted?

Assembly Score reflects confidence in how chains interact and are arranged together. Higher values suggest greater confidence in the predicted assembly.
